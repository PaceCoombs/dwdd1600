Digital Citizenship

What is Digital Citizenship?

Digital citizenship is the responsible, safe, and respectful use of technology and the internet, involving ethical behavior, critical thinking, and understanding your rights and responsibilities in online communities.

What is Digital Law?

Digital law (or cyber law) is the body of legal rules and principles governing activities, rights, and responsibilities in the digital world, regulating online behavior, data, technology use, and electronic transactions to address challenges like privacy, security, intellectual property, and cybercrime. It essentially covers what's legal and illegal online, applying real-world laws to the internet and digital environments.

What is AUP?

AUP most commonly stands for Acceptable Use Policy, a set of rules defining permitted and prohibited uses of an organization's network, devices, and data, ensuring security, compliance, and responsible behavior.

What is Plagiarism?

Plagiarism is presenting someone else's words, ideas, or work as your own without giving proper credit, essentially committing intellectual theft by failing to cite the original source.

What is Academic Dishonesty?

Academic dishonesty is any act that gives a student an unfair advantage in learning or assessment.

What are the Rules for AI Generated Content?

Transparency is required when using AI-generated materials. Any published content generated or substantially influenced* by AI should include an explicit disclosure, clearly visible to the audience. Examples: Label AI-generated illustrations or visuals with a brief disclaimer.
